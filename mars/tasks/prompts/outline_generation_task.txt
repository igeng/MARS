Based on the domain analysis, paper search, deep analysis, connection analysis,
and quality evaluation results, generate a **detailed hierarchical outline**
for an academic literature review on:

{topic}

【Outline Requirements】
1. Follow the standard survey structure:
   - Title
   - Abstract
   - 1. Introduction
   - 2. Background / Preliminaries
   - 3. Core Methods / Techniques (2-4 subsections)
   - 4. Comparative Analysis
   - 5. Open Problems and Challenges
   - 6. Future Research Directions
   - 7. Conclusion
   - References

2. Each section (3-6) must have 2-4 subsections.
3. Max depth: 3 levels (section → subsection → sub-subsection).
4. Every subsection must name specific papers that will be discussed there.
5. Estimated word count per section (total ~3500 words):
   - Abstract: ~200 words
   - Introduction: ~500 words
   - Background: ~500 words
   - Core Methods: ~1000 words (split across subsections)
   - Comparative Analysis: ~500 words
   - Open Problems: ~400 words
   - Future Directions: ~400 words
   - Conclusion: ~200 words

6. Mark which papers from the analysis results will be cited in each subsection.
   Use format: [N] Paper Title (Year) — brief note on what aspect to discuss.

【Output Format】
Output the outline in this exact format:

# Review Outline: {topic}

## Section: [Section Name] (~N words)
### Subsection: [Subsection Name]
- Key papers: [N] Title (Year), [M] Title (Year)
- Key points to cover: ...

### Subsection: [Subsection Name]
- Key papers: ...
- Key points to cover: ...

(Repeat for all sections)

At the end, include:
## Outline Statistics
- Total sections: N
- Total subsections: N
- Max depth: N
- Estimated total words: N
